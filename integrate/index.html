Integrate storage

Add remoteStorage to your app

By making your app support remoteStorage you give your users the choice where to store their personal data. Additionally you don't have to bother implementing your own backend solution. remoteStorage.js is a JavaScript library that does the heavy-lifting of connecting to any remoteStorage backend, caching, synchronizing and storing user data.

Before starting to code you should take care of a few general aspects:

Let your users connect

The remoteStorage widget allows your users to connect to their personal storage server. By default it is part of every app that uses remoteStorage.js. It thus serves as an indicator for users to immediately see that they can use their own storage with an app.

Find out more about integrating the widget in the API docs: displayWidget

Define your data

The next step is to decide what data you want to store from your app. This is done by using a module. You can either choose from a variety of common and custom modules that already exist, or build your own. For each module that your app uses, you need to claim access. The user will see which modules your app requests access to, when connecting to their remoteStorage server.

Find out more: claimAccess, defineModule, BaseClient
